Climate change has gradually increased the frequency and intensity of natural disasters, such as hurricanes tropical storms. Thus, Windborne's weather balloons are crucial to predicting the conditions leading to the development and landfall of these hurricanes.

NOAA's HURDAT2 is a dataset that includes decades of hurricane and tropical storm landfall information. This ML project uses recent hurricane data and weather balloon position information to identify key relationships between the balloons' positions and the hurricane's conditions. The goal is to identify where weather balloons should be concentrated and whether there is a correlation between balloon position and hurricane wind speeds.

üß™ Methodology

  • Fetched each balloon's latitude, longitude, and altitude positions through Windborne's live constellation API.
  • Filtered through NOAA's HURDAT2 dataset to include hurricanes from 2020 to 2023 for maximum relevancy.
  • Matched balloon positions to hurricane positions based on proximity (1‚Äì7 degrees) to identify priority areas for weather balloon placement.
  • Trained a Random Forest model to predict hurricane wind speed from balloon proximity and altitude
